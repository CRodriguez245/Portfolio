Summary

I led design strategy for a rapidly growing healthcare research program supporting 40+ underrepresented high school students.

As the program doubled in size over the past year, I used systems mapping and curriculum design to resolve bottlenecks, clarify team roles, and reframe cohort grouping around student needs. These interventions resulted in smoother delivery, more confident students, and a repeatable framework now adopted across cohorts.

Scaling equity-focused programs requires more than adding seats. It demands rethinking structure, roles, and rhythms to keep students supported as the system grows.

Challenge

NM Scholars empowers first-generation and underrepresented high school students to conduct real healthcare research. But as enrollment scaled, we faced structural breakdowns: fragmented communication, inconsistent instruction, unclear mentor roles, and disengagement from students in larger cohorts.

Solution

I led a systems-level design response to improve clarity, collaboration, and scalability. Key interventions included:

  • Systems mapping of all program actors (students, APCs, mentors, instructors) to identify gaps in responsibility and timing
  • Cohort grouping redesign, shifting from static groupings to interest-based and skill-balanced pods
  • Slide + poster card templates to reduce prep anxiety and increase consistency across student deliverables
  • A rubric-aligned feedback system to clarify APC expectations and reduce weekly review friction
  • A milestone-based schedule and visual planning board to coordinate across instructional leads

Impact

  • Reduced instructional misalignment by formalizing roles across teams
  • Increased student participation in peer critique by 50%
  • Cohort restructuring resulted in more equitable workload distribution
  • Framework now used to onboard new instructors and scale to future NM Scholar cohorts
  • +35% improvement in post-program student clarity scores (schedule, expectations, roles)

Key Decisions

  • Scoped GPT prompts to build confidence, not just speed
  • Removed open-ended tasks after testing showed cognitive overload
  • Integrated reflection questions to surface ethical reasoning
